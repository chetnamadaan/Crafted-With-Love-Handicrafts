World's oldest crafts are here for you..

Dhokra represents one of the ancient methods of metal casting in India, with roots extending over 4,000 years back to the Indus Valley Civilization era. This traditional craft utilizes the lost wax technique, also known as ‘cire-perdue,’ which has been employed by artisans in regions such as Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Orissa, and West Bengal. Each piece is intricately handmade through a complex 13-stage procedure, embodying the legacy and craftsmanship passed down through the ages. The final product, known as Dhokra or Bell metal, is a brass-based alloy combined with other metals, giving it a characteristic antique look.
